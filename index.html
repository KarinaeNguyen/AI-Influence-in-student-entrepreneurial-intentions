1. Variable Definitions & Theory

Path A: The Growth Path

    [cite_start]
  • AI-Enabled Competence (IV): The perception that AI tools enhance efficiency and skill, satisfying the SDT need for Competence [cite: 508].
  • Harmonious Passion (Mediator): Entrepreneurship is internalized autonomously into the student's identity. [cite_start]They engage because they love it [cite: 25, 1293].

Path B: The Pressure Path

    [cite_start]
  • AI-Induced Dependency (IV): The feeling that one cannot function without AI, thwarting the SDT need for Autonomy and creating external pressure [cite: 1294].
    • [cite_start]
  • Obsessive Passion (Mediator): Entrepreneurship is pursued due to internal or external pressure (e.g., fear of missing out, status) [cite: 102, 529].

Contextual Factors

  • Student Hardiness (Moderator): A personality trait comprising Control, Commitment, and Challenge. [cite_start]High hardiness helps Vietnamese students navigate transitional stress [cite: 849, 858].
    • [cite_start]
  • Entrepreneurial Intention (DV): The outcome variable representing the conscious resolve to start a business [cite: 1283].

2. Research Hypotheses

  • H1: AI-Enabled Competence is positively related to Harmonious Passion.
  • H2: AI-Induced Dependency is positively related to Obsessive Passion.
  • H3: Harmonious Passion positively predicts Entrepreneurial Intention (with higher sustainability).
  • H4: Obsessive Passion positively predicts Entrepreneurial Intention (but is prone to conflict).
  • H5: Student Hardiness moderates the relationship between Passion and Intention (strengthening resilience).

3. Methodology: Mixed-Methods

This study utilizes an Explanatory Sequential Design:

  1. Phase 1 (Quantitative): Survey 200-300 Vietnamese students using validated scales (e.g., Vallerand’s Passion Scale, Bartone’s Hardiness Scale). Analyze using SEM.
  2. Phase 2 (Qualitative): Interview outliers to explain how AI fosters dependency vs. competence.

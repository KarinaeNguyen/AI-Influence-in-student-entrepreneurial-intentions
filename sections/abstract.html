The Dual-Edged Sword of AI: Influencing Student Entrepreneurial Passion in Vietnam

Background: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming the entrepreneurial landscape in transitional economies like Vietnam. While AI enhances operational efficiency, its psychological impact on the motivational drivers of student entrepreneurs remains underexplored.

Objective: Grounded in Self-Determination Theory (SDT) and the Dualistic Model of Passion (DMP), this study investigates how AI usage influences the formation of Harmonious and Obsessive passion, and subsequently, Entrepreneurial Intention.

Methodology: We employ a Mixed-Methods Explanatory Sequential Design. First, a quantitative survey (N=300) validates a structural model proposing that AI-Enabled Competence fosters Harmonious Passion, whereas AI-Induced Dependency (thwarting Autonomy) fuels Obsessive Passion. Second, qualitative interviews explore how student Hardiness (Commitment, Control, Challenge) moderates these relationships.

Implications: This research aims to provide empirical evidence on whether AI acts as a resource for sustainable motivation or a pressure causing rigid persistence, offering crucial insights for educators in Vietnamâ€™s innovation-driven economy.

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Dualistic Passion, Entrepreneurial Intention, Self-Determination Theory, Student Hardiness, Vietnam.
